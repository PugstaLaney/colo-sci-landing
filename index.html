The process

From question to hypothesis in four steps

Step 01

🔬

Ask your question

Describe your research direction in plain English. Colo translates it into optimized PubMed search terms automatically.

Step 02

📚

Build your corpus

We pull thousands of peer-reviewed abstracts from PubMed and rank them by citation impact using NIH iCite scores.

Step 03

⚖️

Agents debate

Two expert AI personas — a clinician and a bench researcher — debate your topic across multiple turns, pulling evidence at each step.

Step 04

💡

Get a hypothesis

The agents reach a consensus verdict — an evidence-graded, testable hypothesis with citation support and confidence level.

What makes Colo different

Built for how science actually works

Adversarial reasoning

Two agents with different research perspectives stress-test every claim before a verdict is produced. Conclusions survive scrutiny because they had to earn it.

Citation quality weighting

Retrieved papers are ranked by NIH Relative Citation Ratio — a field-normalized impact score that goes beyond raw citation counts.

Evidence hierarchy tagging

Every claim is tagged by evidence type: [RCT], [META], [COHORT], [PRECLINICAL], or [EXPERT]. You always know what kind of evidence is behind a statement.

Any life science domain

Built on PubMed's full index. Oncology, neuroscience, immunology, pharmacology — if it's in the literature, Colo can synthesize it.

Your corpus, your question

Each session builds a custom knowledge base from your specific research question. Not a generic chatbot — a purpose-built synthesis engine.

Inference clearly marked

Agent reasoning is always prefixed with INFERENCE: so you can distinguish what the literature says from what the agents are interpreting.

Colo is a research ideation tool. Outputs are not medical advice and should not be used as a substitute for professional clinical judgment or peer-reviewed publication.
